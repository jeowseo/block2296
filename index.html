BLOCK2296|BROOKLYN





Block 2296 is located in the North part of Williamsburg Brooklyn. It is surrounded by Wythe Avenue, Berry Street, North 11th Street, and North 10th Street. This block has largely two structures. The west half is old Hecla Iron Works Building (100 N 11th Street), and Vice Media and its sub-companies building (90 N 11th Street).

INDUSTRIAL STRUCTURES





Williamsburg was home of manufacturing starting from 19th Century. Since this block is located in the vicinity of the East River Waterfront and most of the manufacturing businesses back then used maritime transportation, all the building structures were constructed in late 19th century (PLUTO, NYC Department of City Planning). Hecla Iron Works Building was built in 1894. It is now designated as a landmark. The company was a main architectural ornament supplier for many of the cast iron buildings in New York City (Landmark Preservation Commission, 2004).
